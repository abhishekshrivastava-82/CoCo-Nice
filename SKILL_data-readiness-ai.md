# Data Readiness for AI

## Overview

This skill guides organizations in transforming fragmented raw data into operational AI systems using four foundational pillars:

1. Unified Data Infrastructure (Foundation)
2. Semantic & Context Layer (Brain)
3. Data Quantification & Observability (Trust)
4. Agentic Governance & Security (Guardrails)

The goal is to enable AI maturity and operational AI deployment.

---

## Session Prerequisites (Always First)

Before applying any pillar, validate foundational readiness:

1. Identify current data landscape (sources, formats, silos)
2. Confirm Snowflake or data platform availability
3. Assess AI maturity level (PoC, Pilot, Production)

Checkpoint:
"It sounds like you're trying to move from [current state] to AI-driven operations. Is that correct?"

---

## Routing Principles

1. Foundation first – Always start with data infrastructure
2. Context before intelligence – AI fails without semantics
3. Trust before scale – Observability is mandatory
4. Guardrails before autonomy – Governance enables agents
5. Operationalization is the end goal – Not just PoCs

---

## Pillar 1: Unified Data Infrastructure (The Foundation)

### Objective
Create a single, scalable data backbone for all AI workloads.

### Capabilities

- Unified Lakehouse (Data Lake + Warehouse)
- Multimodal Ingestion (structured, unstructured)
- Vectorization Strategy for GenAI
- Multi-model storage (tables, documents, embeddings)

---

## Pillar 2: Semantic & Context Layer (The Brain)

### Objective
Bridge data to meaning so AI systems can reason, not just retrieve.

### Capabilities

- Centralized semantic layer
- Knowledge graphs
- Metadata enrichment
- Business metric standardization

---

## Pillar 3: Data Quantification & Observability (The Trust)

### Objective
Ensure data reliability and fitness for AI models.

### Capabilities

- Automated feature stores
- Data quality monitoring
- Anomaly & drift detection
- Lineage tracking

---

## Pillar 4: Agentic Governance & Security (The Guardrails)

### Objective
Enable safe and scalable AI agents with control mechanisms.

### Capabilities

- Identity for AI agents
- Dynamic masking & redaction
- Policy-as-code
- Compliance automation

---

## Final Stage: Operational AI Hub

### Objective
Activate AI across the enterprise.

### Components

- AI Models (ML + GenAI)
- AI Workflows & Agents
- Decision automation systems

---

## End-to-End Flow

Raw Data → Unified Infrastructure → Semantic Layer → Observability → Governance → AI Hub

---

## Key Principle

AI success is not model-first. It is data readiness first.
